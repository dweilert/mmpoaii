Communication Templates Guide

Board Member Operational Reference — Complete enforcement sequences, assessment collection procedures, ARC timelines, delivery methods, record retention requirements, and compliance checklists.

Two Types of Templates
MANDATORY Templates (16) DISCRETIONARY Templates (11)
These templates are required by Texas law or the CC&Rs before the Association may take a specific action. Skipping them, sending by the wrong delivery method, or omitting required content can void the action entirely — meaning a lien is unenforceable, a fine is void, or an ARC decision is invalid. These templates reflect best practice and community governance standards. They are not required by statute but are expected by Members and protect the Board from claims of unfair or inconsistent administration. Skipping them will not void an action but may damage community trust and expose the Board to criticism.

The Enforcement Sequence

8-Step Process from Notice to Self-Help or Legal Action

This is the mandatory sequence for enforcing deed restrictions against a violating property. Every step must be followed in order. Skipping steps or using the wrong template can render the enforcement void.

Step Template Action Required If Skipped
1 T7 Courtesy Notice (optional informal notice). Send written notice, give owner chance to self-correct. —
2 T8 Notice of Violation. Send by CERTIFIED MAIL with 5 required elements: (a) violation description, (b) cure period, (c) 30-day hearing right, (d) attorney fee warning, (e) SCRA notice. Fine void; enforcement invalid
3 — Wait for cure period. Allow time stated in T8 for owner to remedy. If owner requests hearing within 30 days, go to Step 4. —
4 T9 Hearing Notice. If owner requests hearing, send T9 with 10+ days advance notice, names of 3-member panel, right to counsel. Hearing void
5 — Conduct Hearing. Panel of 3+ Board members. Allow owner to present evidence. Keep minutes. —
6 T10 Hearing Decision. Issue written decision within 14 days. State finding, fine if any, remedy deadline. No enforceable fine
7 T11 Remedy Notice. If uncured after decision, send T11 with 30-day final remedy deadline and self-help warning. Self-help entry = trespass
8 — Self-help or legal action. If still uncured after T11, Board may enter property or pursue legal remedies (collection agency, attorney). —
Key Timing Rules
  • Cure period minimum: 10 days for most violations (check CC&Rs)
  • Hearing request window: 30 days from T8 Notice of Violation
  • Hearing notice lead time: 10 days advance notice minimum
  • Hearing decision deadline: 14 days after hearing concludes
  • Remedy notice cure: 30 days from T11 to cure
  • Fine cap: $200/day continuing; $5,000 total per violation

The Assessment Collection Sequence

8-Step Process from Delinquency to Lien Filing

This sequence must be followed exactly to create an enforceable assessment lien. The most common mistake is filing a lien without sending the Pre-Lien Notice (T5) by certified mail.

Step Template Action Required If Skipped
1 T26 Adopt Collection Policy. Before any collection action, adopt and distribute written policy to all members (required by § 209.0093). Collection invalid
2 — Assessment becomes delinquent. Interest at 10% per annum from due date. Document in records. —
3 — Optional reminder. Send informal payment reminder (not required, does not satisfy any statute). —
4 T3 Payment Plan Offer. Send by certified mail offering minimum 3-month plan BEFORE filing any lien (§ 209.0093). Lien challengeable
5 T4 If accepted: Payment Plan Agreement. Execute signed agreement. Defer lien filing while payments current. —
6 T5 Pre-Lien Notice. Send by CERTIFIED MAIL with 6 required elements: total amount, itemized breakdown, payment address, deadline, right to cure, right to payment plan. Lien is VOID
7 — File assessment lien. Record in Travis County (property records) if not cured within T5 deadline (typically 30 days). —
8 T6 Lien Release. Within 30 days of full payment, record release in Travis County and send T6 confirmation to owner. Potential legal action
Homestead Foreclosure Rules
A homestead cannot be foreclosed to satisfy HOA assessment liens UNLESS: (a) delinquent amount exceeds $2,500 (excluding interest/fees), AND (b) foreclosure is judicial (not non-judicial/power of sale). Most HOA liens are non-judicial and cannot foreclose homesteads regardless of amount.

The ARC Application Timeline

4-Step Process for Architectural Review Approvals

The ARC (Architectural Review Committee) must follow these strict timelines. Missing any deadline can result in deemed approval or void denials.

Step Template Action Required Risk If Late
1 T17 Acknowledgment of Receipt. Within 5 business days of receiving complete application, send T17 starting the 45-day clock. 45-day clock unclear
2 T18 Notice of Incompleteness (if needed). Within 10 business days if submission incomplete. List every deficiency with specific details. Clock may continue running
3 — ARC Review. 45-day review period from acknowledged complete receipt. Request additional info, conduct site visit, evaluate compliance. —
4 T19/T20/T21 Decision Notice. Issue Approval (T19), Conditional Approval (T20), or Denial (T21) within 45 days. Denial requires specific written reasons with alternative options. Denial may be void; deemed approval possible
ARC Denial Requirements
Under § 209.00505, a vague ARC denial without specific features identified as violations and suggested modifications will be voided by a court. Every denied application must include: (1) exact description of what violates CC&Rs, (2) specific alternative designs that would be acceptable, and (3) explanation of why each alternative complies.

Certified Mail Best Practice
If certified mail is returned "unclaimed" or "undeliverable," document the attempt, photograph the returned card, and re-send to the property address via first-class mail AND to the owner's last known address on file with the Travis County Appraisal District (TCAD). This creates evidence of reasonable effort.
Record Retention & Archival

Mandatory Retention Periods by Document Type

Texas law imposes specific retention periods for HOA records. Destroying records before the retention period expires can expose the Board to liability and is grounds for sanctions.

Document Type Retain For Related Templates
Enforcement records (NOVs, hearing transcripts, decisions, fines) 4 years from resolution T7–T11
Assessment and lien records 4 years after lien release (or 4 years if never filed) T3–T6
ARC applications and decisions 4 years from decision date T17–T21
Contracts: 1+ year duration 4 years from contract end —
Contracts: <1 year duration 4 years from expiration —
Financial statements, budgets, tax returns, audit reports 7 years from date of statement T1, T2, T24
Insurance policies and certificates 4 years from expiration —
Board meeting minutes PERMANENT (never destroy) T9–T14
Membership/owner meeting minutes PERMANENT (never destroy) T15, T16
Governing documents (CC&Rs, Bylaws, Rules) PERMANENT — post on website, update online annually T15, T26, T27
Resale certificates & estoppel statements 4 years from issuance T23
Tenant registration forms Duration of tenancy + 1 year T22
Delivery records (certified mail cards, email logs, posting screenshots) 4 years from mailing/delivery date T8–T11, T3–T6, T17–T22
Payment plan agreements 4 years after final payment or plan termination T4
Recommended Practice
Create a Records Retention Matrix and distribute it to all officers. Include a shred-date calendar in the office manager's system. Every document should be labeled with its destruction date. Use secure destruction (shredding) for records containing personal financial information.
Top 10 Board Compliance Mistakes

Common errors and how to avoid them

Sending NOV by regular mail instead of certified mail
Always use certified mail (Return Receipt) for T8 Notice of Violation. Keep green card and tracking number.
Imposing fine before giving 30-day hearing opportunity
Wait minimum 30 days after T8 before levying any fine. Only issue fine after hearing (T10) or if owner waives hearing in writing.
Filing lien without Pre-Lien Notice (T5)
NEVER file lien until T5 is sent by certified mail AND cure period expires. Missing T5 makes lien VOID and unenforceable.
Filing lien without offering payment plan (T3)
Send T3 Payment Plan Offer by certified mail BEFORE sending T5. Build this into collection calendar. § 209.0093 requires it.
Board meetings without 72-hour notice
Post T12 Board Meeting Notice on website + email owners every time, no exceptions. 72 hours minimum (check bylaws for more).
Fines before Fine Schedule published
Adopt T27 Fine Schedule at a Board meeting and post on website BEFORE levying any fine. Fines without published schedule are unenforceable.
ARC denial without written specific reasons
Always use T21 Denial with EVERY field completed. Include specific features violated, alternative designs, and compliance explanation. Vague denials are void.
Not distributing Collection Policy (T26) first
Adopt T26 at a Board meeting and distribute to all members BEFORE first collection action. Missing T26 invalidates entire collection sequence.
Altering required statutory language in templates
Only fill in red [BRACKETED] fields with specific facts. Never change black text without attorney review and written approval.
Not completing Delivery Record block
Fill in Delivery Record section the day notice is sent. Include method, date, recipient, tracking number. Keep with original letter in file.
