Assessment Payment Plan

Your right to a structured payment arrangement before the Association may file an assessment lien — defined in the CC&Rs and required by Texas law.

📋 CC&Rs Art. IV § 14 · Texas Property Code § 209.0093
📌

A Payment Plan IS Defined in Your CC&Rs

The Association is required by both the CC&Rs and Texas state law to offer you a payment plan before filing a lien.

After a thorough review of the MMPOAII CC&Rs and Bylaws, a formal payment plan is expressly defined in the Declaration of Covenants, Conditions, and Restrictions. Article IV, Section 14 — titled "Assessment Payment Plans" — establishes the minimum terms that govern any payment arrangement between a delinquent Owner and the Association.

This plan is not discretionary. Under Texas Property Code § 209.0093, the Association must offer a reasonable payment plan before it may file an assessment lien against your property. The CC&Rs mirror this requirement and add specific protections for owners.

Statutory Basis — Tex. Prop. Code § 209.0093

A property owners' association that is subject to Chapter 209 must offer a delinquent property owner a reasonable payment plan before the association may file an assessment lien. Failure to offer a plan, or filing a lien before the plan opportunity is given, renders the lien potentially unenforceable.

CC&Rs — Article IV, Section 14

The exact terms as written in the Declaration of Covenants, Conditions & Restrictions.

The following is the verbatim text of Section 14 from Article IV of the MMPOAII CC&Rs (V12), governing assessment payment plans:

CC&Rs — Art. IV § 14 Assessment Payment Plans

Consistent with Texas Property Code § 209.0093, the Association shall offer a delinquent Owner a reasonable payment plan before filing an assessment lien. The following minimum terms apply to any payment plan offered by the Association:

  1. the payment plan shall allow the Owner to pay the delinquent assessments, accrued interest, and administrative costs in equal monthly installments over a period of not less than three (3) months and not more than twelve (12) months, as agreed between the Owner and the Board;
  2. interest shall accrue on the unpaid balance during the payment plan period at the rate specified in Article IV, Section 8 of this Declaration (10% per annum), or such lower rate as the Board may establish by resolution;
  3. the Owner must make all installment payments by the due dates specified in the payment plan agreement;
  4. if the Owner fails to make any installment payment within fifteen (15) days of its due date, the payment plan is void, the full remaining balance becomes immediately due, and the Association may proceed with lien filing and other collection remedies without further notice;
  5. the Board may require the Owner to execute a written payment plan agreement as a condition of deferring lien filing; and
  6. an Owner who fulfills a payment plan in full is not subject to a lien filing for the delinquency covered by that plan.

The Board may adopt supplemental payment plan policies consistent with this Section and applicable Texas law.

Interest Rate Source — Art. IV § 8

The 10% per annum interest rate is set in Article IV, Section 8 ("Effect of Nonpayment of Assessments"), which states: any assessment not paid within thirty (30) days after the due date bears interest from the due date at 10% per annum, or the maximum rate permitted by Texas law, whichever is less. The Board may set a lower rate by resolution for any given payment plan.

How to Request a Payment Plan

Step-by-step process for submitting H9 and what to expect at each stage.

Use template H9 — Payment Plan Request — to formally request a payment arrangement. Submitting H9 proactively, before the Association sends a pre-lien notice, demonstrates good faith and may prevent lien proceedings from starting.

1
Identify your balance
Contact the Association or review your account to confirm the total delinquent amount, including any accrued interest (10% annually from the missed due date) and any administrative fees already assessed.
2
Complete and submit H9
Download the H9 Payment Plan Request template. Include your preferred plan duration (3–12 months) and proposed monthly amount. Send by certified mail with return receipt to create a dated record of your request.
3
Board reviews and responds
The Board must offer a plan of at least 3 months (§ 209.0093). They may propose different terms within the 3–12 month window. Respond promptly — the lien clock continues running until a plan is agreed and signed.
4
Execute the written agreement
The Board may require a written T4 Payment Plan Agreement as a condition of deferring lien filing (CC&Rs Art. IV § 14(e)). Review, sign, and keep a copy. The agreement specifies the monthly amount, due dates, and consequences of default.
5
Make payments on time
Pay each installment by the date specified in the agreement. You have a 15-day grace period — but do not rely on it. Missing by even one day beyond day 15 voids the plan and the full balance becomes immediately due.
6
Obtain confirmation of completion
Once your final installment is paid, request written confirmation that the delinquency is resolved and no lien will be filed. Keep this indefinitely — it is your proof of lien protection under Art. IV § 14(f).
Submit H9 before you receive a pre-lien notice (T5). Once the Association sends T5, the formal collection clock has already started. Requesting a plan early gives you more negotiating room on plan duration and terms.

Plan Terms in Detail

Full breakdown of each provision — what it means for you as an owner.

Duration: 3 to 12 Months

The plan must cover a minimum of 3 months and a maximum of 12 months. The exact length is agreed between you and the Board. Longer plans mean smaller monthly payments but more total interest accrued. Use the table below to estimate your options:

Balance Owed 3-Month Plan 6-Month Plan 12-Month Plan
$500 ~$168 / mo ~$86 / mo ~$44 / mo
$1,000 ~$336 / mo ~$172 / mo ~$88 / mo
$2,000 ~$672 / mo ~$344 / mo ~$176 / mo
$3,000 ~$1,008 / mo ~$516 / mo ~$264 / mo

Estimates include approximate 10% annual interest. Actual amounts will vary based on your specific balance and the plan start date. Request an itemized statement from the Association before signing.

Interest Rate: 10% Per Annum

Interest accrues on the unpaid balance at 10% per year throughout the plan period. This is the same rate that began accruing when your assessment first became delinquent (30 days past due). The Board may reduce this rate by formal resolution — you may request a reduced rate when submitting H9.

Equal Monthly Installments

All payments must be in equal monthly amounts. You cannot make variable payments (e.g., pay more one month and less the next). The written agreement will state the exact dollar amount and due date for each installment.

What Is Included in the Plan Balance

  • 💵 Delinquent assessments — the overdue Annual or Special Assessment principal
  • 📈 Accrued interest — 10% per annum from the date each assessment was due
  • 📋 Administrative costs — any fees the Association has incurred in connection with the delinquency (late fees, mailing costs, etc.)
  • ⚠️ Not included: Attorney's fees and lien filing fees that have not yet been incurred — these are avoided by entering a plan before a lien is filed
Entering a Plan Early Saves You Money

Attorney's fees for lien filing and collection can add hundreds or thousands of dollars to your balance. Requesting a plan with H9 before the Association files a lien keeps those costs off your account entirely.

Default: What Happens If You Miss a Payment

The 15-day grace period, what "void" means, and how to protect yourself.

The CC&Rs include a strict default clause in Art. IV § 14(d). Understanding it is critical before entering a plan.

Default Clause — Art. IV § 14(d)

If the Owner fails to make any installment payment within fifteen (15) days of its due date, the payment plan is void, the full remaining balance becomes immediately due, and the Association may proceed with lien filing and other collection remedies without further notice.

What "Void" Means in Practice

✓ Making Payments on Time

Each payment made by the due date (or within the 15-day grace window) keeps the plan active. The Association cannot file a lien while the plan is in good standing. Your lien protection remains intact.

✗ Missing a Payment

If you miss day 15 after a payment due date, the plan is automatically void. The entire remaining balance — not just the missed installment — becomes due immediately. The Association can file a lien without sending any additional notices.

The 15-Day Grace Period

The CC&Rs provide a 15-day window after each payment due date. This is a grace period — not an extension. Best practice is to pay several days before the due date, not the last possible day, to account for processing time, holidays, and mail delays.

Automate your payments if possible. Set up automatic bill pay through your bank for the exact plan amount to arrive a few days before the due date. One missed auto-draft can still trigger default if not caught within 15 days.

If You Fall Behind on a Plan Payment

  1. Act immediately — contact the Association in writing the same day you realize a payment was late or missed.
  2. Make the payment — if you are still within the 15-day grace window, pay immediately and document it.
  3. Request a new plan — if the 15 days have passed and the plan is voided, you may submit a new H9 requesting a revised plan. The Association is not required to offer a second plan, but it may do so at its discretion.
  4. Submit H11 — if you believe any amount in the plan balance is incorrect, dispute it with H11 while simultaneously addressing the payment issue.

Lien Protection & Your Rights

How successfully completing a plan permanently protects you from a lien for that delinquency.

The CC&Rs and Texas law together provide strong lien protections when you engage the payment plan process correctly.

Protection 1 — Right to a Plan Before Any Lien

Under § 209.0093, the Association must offer you a payment plan before filing an assessment lien. The pre-lien notice (T5, sent by certified mail) must expressly inform you of your right to request a plan. If the Association files a lien without offering you this opportunity, the lien may be unenforceable.

Protection 2 — No Lien for Completed Plans

Art. IV § 14(f) states clearly: "an Owner who fulfills a payment plan in full is not subject to a lien filing for the delinquency covered by that plan." Once your final payment clears, the Association's right to file a lien for that specific delinquency is extinguished.

Get Written Confirmation

After making your final plan payment, send a letter to the Association (or use H20 General Inquiry) requesting written confirmation that the plan has been fulfilled and no lien will be filed. Keep this letter indefinitely with your property records.

Protection 3 — Lien Foreclosure Threshold

Even if a lien is filed, the Association cannot foreclose on your homestead unless the total delinquent assessments (excluding interest, attorney's fees, and costs) exceed $2,500, as required by § 209.0091. This gives you additional time to resolve a delinquency before facing the most severe remedy.

Protection 4 — Right to Dispute the Balance

You have the right to challenge any assessment amount you believe is incorrect using H11 (Assessment Dispute / Clarification). You should submit H11 separately and concurrently if you believe the balance in the plan is wrong — disputing the balance does not pause the delinquency clock, but it creates a record of your objection.

Summary of Your Rights in the Collection Process

  • 📬Right to a pre-lien notice by certified mail stating the total amount, itemized breakdown, and payment address (§ 209.023)
  • 📋Right to be informed of your right to a payment plan in every pre-lien notice
  • 🤝Right to a minimum 3-month payment plan before any lien may be filed (§ 209.0093 / CC&Rs Art. IV § 14)
  • 🔔Right to a hearing before any fine is imposed for a violation (§ 209.006 / § 209.007)
  • 📁Right to inspect Association records including your account ledger (§ 209.005 / H8)
  • 🛡️Right to be free of lien foreclosure if the delinquent principal is $2,500 or less (§ 209.0091)

Owner Checklist — Payment Plan

Everything you need to do to successfully request, enter, and complete a payment plan.

Before You Submit H9

  • Request and review an itemized statement of your account balance (use H8 if needed)
  • Verify all amounts are correct — dispute any errors with H11 concurrently
  • Calculate what monthly payment you can realistically make over 3–12 months
  • Determine whether you want to propose 3, 6, or 12 months in your H9

When Submitting H9

  • Complete all fields in the H9 template including your proposed duration and monthly amount
  • Send by certified mail with return receipt to the Association's official address
  • Keep a copy of the signed H9 and the certified mail receipt
  • Note the date sent — this starts the record of your proactive good-faith effort

After the Plan is Agreed

  • Review the T4 Payment Plan Agreement carefully before signing
  • Confirm the monthly amount, exact due dates, and total number of payments
  • Note the 15-day grace period for each payment — but do not rely on it
  • Set up automated bill pay or calendar reminders for each payment date
  • Keep a copy of the signed T4 agreement

During the Plan

  • Pay each installment on time — at least 2–3 business days before the due date
  • Save payment confirmation (bank receipt, check copy, or online confirmation) for every installment
  • If you anticipate a problem with any payment, contact the Association in writing immediately

After the Final Payment

  • Confirm your final payment has been received and applied by the Association
  • Request written confirmation that the plan is fulfilled and no lien will be filed (Art. IV § 14(f))
  • File all documents — H9, T4, payment records, and confirmation letter — with your property records
