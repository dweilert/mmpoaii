Federal law — overrides all state and local rules 6 provisions
Fair Housing Act (FHA)
Anti-discrimination in housing & HOA rule enforcement. Cannot be waived by any CC&R.
Federal
FDCPA (15 U.S.C. §1692)
Limits third-party collectors hired by HOA. Debt validation, no harassment, no false statements.
Federal
ADA / FHA Amendments Act
Reasonable accommodation for disability — overrides CC&R provisions that deny access.
Federal
Bankruptcy Code (11 U.S.C.)
Automatic stay immediately halts all HOA collection, lien enforcement & foreclosure.
Federal
Servicemembers Civil Relief Act (SCRA)
Active-duty military receive foreclosure & collection protections. HOA must halt proceedings.
Federal
Secretary of State / IRS
HOA non-profit incorporation with TX Secretary of State; IRS 501(c)(4) tax-exempt status.
Federal/State
sets a floor — Texas law cannot go below this
Texas Constitution — foundational state protections 3 provisions
Art. XVI §50 — Homestead Protection
HOA must get a court order to foreclose on a homestead. Direct source of §209.0092.
TX Constitution
Art. I §19 — Due Course of Law
No property deprivation without due process. Constitutional basis for all §209.006 notice requirements.
TX Constitution
Art. I §17 — Taking of Property
Private property shall not be taken for public use without just compensation. Limits HOA easement authority.
TX Constitution
state statutes must conform to the constitution

Corporate, Tax & Debt Collection
Bus. Org. Code Ch. 22
Non-profit corp law. Board fiduciary duties, liability protection, corporate formalities.
All HOAs
Bus. Org. Code Ch. 251
Texas Cooperative Association Act. Governs co-ops that function like HOAs but are structured as cooperatives.
Co-ops
Finance Code Ch. 392 (TDCA)
Texas Debt Collection Act. Applies alongside FDCPA to HOA management companies and in-house collection.
All HOAs

Government Transparency & Political Rights
Gov. Code Ch. 551 — Open Meetings
Open Meetings Act. §209.0051 carves out large HOAs governed by §552.0036. Rarely applies but important when it does.
Large HOAs only
Gov. Code Ch. 552 — Public Info Act
§209.005 carves out HOAs subject to §552.0036. HOAs meeting that threshold become quasi-public bodies.
Large HOAs only
Election Code §259.002
HOA cannot prohibit political signs from 90 days before to 10 days after an election. Works with §202.009.
All HOAs
Occ. Code / TREC Rules
TREC caps resale cert fees ($375 cert / $75 update) and publishes official forms for Ch. 207 compliance.
Disclosure
litigation, limitations & remedies available alongside Property Code rights
Texas Civil Practice & Remedies Code + Local Gov. Code — enforcement & procedure 5 provisions
CPRC §16.035
4-year statute of limitations on real property liens — HOA has 4 years to foreclose before the lien expires.
Limitations
CPRC Ch. 65 — Injunctions
TRO / Temporary Injunction to stop a wrongful foreclosure sale. Homeowner's fastest emergency legal tool.
Remedies
CPRC Ch. 154 — ADR
Alternative Dispute Resolution framework. §209.007 references ADR — this chapter sets the rules and procedures.
All Disputes
CPRC Ch. 38 — Attorney's Fees
Statutory basis for recovering attorney's fees in contract & property disputes. Supplements §209.008.
Litigation
Local Gov. Code §§192–193
County clerk recording requirements. Liens and dedicatory instruments must be properly recorded here to be legally effective.
County Clerks
all layers above set the outer limits for Texas Property Code
Texas Property Code — Title 5 (Exempt Property & Liens) — lien mechanics 2 chapters
Ch. 41 — Homestead Exemptions
Property Code definition of "homestead." Implements Art. XVI §50 in statute form. Defines what qualifies for homestead foreclosure protection under §209.0092.
Homestead
Ch. 51 — Lien Foreclosure Procedures
The actual mechanics of non-judicial foreclosure: courthouse-steps sale, posting & filing of notice, trustee sale rules. Used by HOAs after a §209.0092 court order authorizes the sale.
Foreclosure Mechanics
Title 11 builds on the lien and homestead framework established in Title 5
Texas Property Code — Title 11 (Restrictive Covenants) — the HOA-specific statutes 15+ chapters
Foundational chapters — apply broadly across subdivisions
Ch. 201 — Definitions
Dictionary for all of Title 11. Defines "restrictions" and "subdivision."
Foundation
★ Ch. 202 — Restrictive Covenants
Applies to ALL Texas subdivisions. Defines dedicatory instrument. Requires county recordation. Prohibits many restrictive covenant types.
Applies to all
Ch. 203 — Certain Counties
Land use restriction enforcement in specific Texas counties. Supplements Ch. 202 in those jurisdictions.
Select Counties
Ch. 204 — Assessment Powers
Assessment authority for subdivisions outside Ch. 209. Defines "regular" and "special" assessment used by Ch. 207.
Assessments
Ch. 205 — Revised Subdivisions
Restrictive covenants applicable to revised or replatted subdivisions in certain counties.
Select Counties
Ch. 206 — Extension of Restrictions
Extends restrictions imposing regular assessments in certain subdivisions. Prevents restriction expiration.
Extensions
★ Ch. 207 — Disclosure / Resale
Required on every home sale. HOA must deliver CC&Rs, bylaws, rules & resale certificate within 10 business days.
Disclosure
Ch. 208 — Historic Neighborhoods
Amendment and termination of restrictive covenants in designated historic neighborhoods. Special procedures apply.
Historic Areas
Ch. 210 — Covenant Extension by Petition
Allows residential covenant extension or modification through petition process in certain subdivisions.
Extensions
Ch. 211 — Certain Subdivision Restrictions
Amendment and enforcement of restrictions in certain subdivisions. Supplement to Ch. 209 in those cases.
Amendments

Condominium chapters — parallel to Ch. 209 but for condos
Ch. 82 — TUCA (condos post-Jan 1, 1994)
Texas Uniform Condominium Act. Ch. 209 equivalent for condos. §82.157 = condo resale certificate. §82.1141 = condo records access.
Condos post-1994
Ch. 81 — Original Condo Act (pre-1994)
Older law for condos with pre-1994 declarations. Less comprehensive than TUCA. Ch. 202 still applies.
Condos pre-1994
Ch. 5 (Conveyances) & Ch. 12 (Recording)
Foundational property law. Deed restriction contract law (Ch. 5) and county deed records recording rules (Ch. 12) that make HOA documents legally effective.

HOA governing documents — your community's own rulebook (most specific layer) 4 documents
Subdivision Plat
Recorded survey map. Defines lots, common areas, easements, setbacks. The first dedicatory instrument.
CC&Rs / Declaration
Founding community contract. Sets assessments, restrictions, interest rates. Must be filed under §202.006.
Bylaws
Internal operating manual: meetings, elections, board powers. Must conform to Ch. 209 & BOC Ch. 22.
Rules & Regulations
Day-to-day rules. Must be adopted via §209.0041 process. Fine schedule required by §209.0061 lives here.
